# 1. Industry Overview

Tourism is one of Morocco’s most strategic economic sectors and a major driver of employment, foreign currency, and regional development.

The sector has experienced rapid growth over the last few years, supported by government investment, expanded international air connectivity, and increased global demand for cultural and experiential travel.

Morocco is positioning itself as a **global tourism hub**, particularly in Africa and the Mediterranean region.

The government is actively pushing tourism development through a national tourism roadmap and infrastructure investments.

The tourism sector currently contributes **around 7% of Morocco’s GDP**, making it one of the largest economic sectors in the country. ([Reuters][1])

---

# 2. Market Size

Tourism demand in Morocco has reached historic levels.

Key indicators:

* **19.8 million tourists visited Morocco in 2025**, a record high. ([Reuters][1])
* Tourism revenue reached **124 billion MAD (~$13.5 billion)** in 2025. ([Reuters][1])
* The tourism and hospitality sector is valued at roughly **$10.5 billion** in market size. ([Ken Research][2])

Tourism growth has been steady:

* 17.4 million tourists in 2024
* 19.8 million in 2025 (≈14% growth) ([Reuters][1])

The government target:

* **26 million tourists annually by 2030**. ([Reuters][1])

This growth will likely accelerate due to Morocco hosting part of the **2030 FIFA World Cup**.

# 5. Tourism Supply Structure

Tourism services in Morocco are highly fragmented.

Most experiences are operated by:

* small agencies
* independent guides
* family-run businesses
* small tour companies

Many operators lack:

* digital booking systems
* structured customer management
* operational management software

This creates inefficiencies in the tourism supply chain.

---

# 6. Government Tourism Strategy

Morocco launched a **national tourism roadmap (2023–2026)** to strengthen the sector and improve competitiveness.

Key objectives:

* strengthen tourism infrastructure
* increase employment
* attract foreign investment
* promote authentic experiences
* modernize tourism services

The roadmap aims to position Morocco among the **top 15 global tourism destinations**. ([Maroc][5])

# 8. Market Opportunities

Based on research, several opportunities exist.

## Tourism Experience Infrastructure

The biggest gap in the market is:

**structuring the experience economy.**

Most local tourism experiences lack:

* booking infrastructure
* digital discovery
* operational coordination tools

---

## AI Trip Planning

Travel planning is still manual and fragmented.

AI-powered itinerary planning could simplify trip organization.

---

## Operator Management Software

* bookings
* schedules
* staff
* logistics

---

* verified guides

* trustworthy operators

# 9. Investment Outlook

Tourism is a priority sector for Moroccan economic growth.

Government and private investment continue to increase in:

* tourism infrastructure
* hospitality
* transportation
* digital transformation

Major events like the **2030 World Cup** are expected to accelerate tourism demand.

---

# Market Research Conclusion

Morocco’s tourism sector is growing rapidly and represents a **multi-billion-dollar opportunity**.

However, the operational layer of tourism experiences remains fragmented and poorly digitized.

There is a clear opportunity to build technology that:

* structures tourism supply
* simplifies discovery and booking
* improves operational coordination

A tourism infrastructure platform could significantly improve efficiency for operators and create a better experience for travelers.
