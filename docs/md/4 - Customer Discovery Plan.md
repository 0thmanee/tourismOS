# Customer Discovery Plan

**Project:** Tourism Experience Infrastructure Platform
**Goal:** Validate the most painful and monetizable problem in Moroccan tourism

---

# 1. Research Goal

The objective is **not to validate your idea**.

The objective is to validate:

> Which exact operational problem is painful enough that Moroccan tourism operators will **pay to solve it immediately**

We are testing these hypotheses:

### Core Hypotheses

H1 — Operators lose bookings due to manual workflows
H2 — WhatsApp-based operations create chaos
H3 — Scheduling and availability management is broken
H4 — Operators lack visibility on their business (no dashboards, no data)
H5 — Tourists struggle with trust and discovery
H6 — Existing tools are too complex or not adapted to local context

These are not guesses — they are derived from real industry pain points:

* lack of real-time visibility
* system complexity
* integration issues
* operational inefficiencies ([ttsltd.com][1])

---

# 2. Target Interview Segments

You should NOT interview “tourism in general”.

You must segment.

---

## Segment 1 — Experience Operators (Primary)

Your most important group.

Target:

* desert tour operators (Merzouga, Agafay)
* quad / buggy operators
* surf schools (Taghazout)
* hiking / trekking guides
* cooking workshop hosts
* cultural guides (Fes, Marrakech)

### Why this segment

They:

* manage bookings daily
* feel operational pain
* lose money from inefficiency
* are your future paying customers

---

## Segment 2 — Small Agencies

Target:

* local tour agencies
* riads organizing experiences
* day-trip agencies

### Why

They sit between:

* tourists
* operators

They experience:

* coordination pain
* supplier chaos
* communication issues

---

## Segment 3 — Tourists (Secondary)

Target:

* recent visitors to Morocco
* digital nomads
* European tourists

### Why

They validate:

* discovery problems
* trust issues
* booking friction

Example real issues:

* scams
* fake guides
* unreliable services ([Morocco Mw Tours][2])

# 4. Interview Method

## Format

* 30–45 minutes
* informal conversation
* NOT a survey
* NOT pitching your idea

---

## Golden Rule

❌ Do NOT say:

> “I’m building a platform…”

✅ Say:

> “I’m studying how tourism businesses work in Morocco”

---

# 5. Interview Questions (Real, Tested Structure)

## Section A — Context

* What type of activities do you offer?
* How many bookings do you get per week?
* Where do your customers come from?

---

## Section B — Workflow

* How do customers contact you?
* What happens from first message → booking?
* How do you track bookings?
* How do you manage availability?

---

## Section C — Pain

This is the most important part.

* What is the most stressful part of your work?
* When do you lose customers?
* Have you ever had double bookings?
* What mistakes happen often?
* What wastes your time every day?

---

## Section D — Tools

* What tools do you use today?
* Do you use any booking system?
* Why / why not?

This validates real insight:

Many systems fail because they are:

* too complex
* not adapted
* poorly integrated ([ttsltd.com][1])

---

## Section E — Money

* Have you ever paid for software?
* Would you pay to fix [problem]?
* How much would that be worth monthly?

---

## Section F — Trust (Tourists + Operators)

* Do customers trust you easily?
* Do tourists hesitate before booking?
* Do scams affect your business?

Important insight:

Trust is a real issue in Morocco:

* scams
* unverified providers
* misleading offers ([Morocco Mw Tours][2])

Also:

* professionals complain about unverified providers on platforms ([Yabiladi][3])

---

# 6. What You Are Looking For

After interviews, you want to identify:

## Repeated patterns

Example:

* “I lose bookings because I reply late”
* “Everything is on WhatsApp”
* “I forget bookings”
* “I don’t know my availability clearly”

---

## High-intensity pain

Not:

> “this would be nice”

But:

> “this is a real problem every week”

---

## Existing workarounds

Gold signal:

If people built hacks like:

* Excel sheets
* notebooks
* multiple phones

→ this means **strong demand**

---

## Willingness to pay

Critical:

If nobody pays → no business.
