# Problem Statement Document

**Project:** AI Tourism Infrastructure Platform (working name)
**Region:** Morocco
**Date:** March 2026

---

# 1. Problem Overview

Morocco is experiencing rapid tourism growth, with millions of visitors annually and significant national investment in tourism infrastructure. However, the **operational layer of tourism experiences remains fragmented and largely manual**.

Most tourism services such as:

* local guides
* desert camps
* activity operators
* cultural workshops
* transport providers
* small tour agencies

still manage bookings, scheduling, customer communication, and logistics through **WhatsApp, phone calls, spreadsheets, and manual coordination**.

This creates operational chaos that leads to:

* lost bookings
* double bookings
* poor communication with tourists
* unreliable scheduling
* missed revenue opportunities

At the same time, tourists struggle to discover authentic experiences because many operators **have no structured online presence or booking infrastructure**.

This gap between **tourism demand and operational supply infrastructure** creates a large opportunity for a modern digital platform.

---

# 2. Target Users

## Primary Users (B2B)

Tourism experience providers operating in Morocco, including:

* desert tour operators
* quad / buggy operators
* surf schools
* hiking guides
* cultural guides
* cooking workshops
* adventure tour providers
* transport operators working with tours
* riads offering local experiences

These businesses are typically:

* small to mid-sized
* digitally under-equipped
* managing operations manually

---

## Secondary Users (B2C)

Tourists visiting Morocco who want to:

* discover authentic experiences
* plan trips easily
* book activities reliably
* avoid scams or unreliable operators

Tourists often rely on:

* travel blogs
* hotel recommendations
* word of mouth
* scattered booking websites

There is no unified system for authentic experience discovery.

---

# 3. Current Workflow (How Things Work Today)

A typical tourism booking process currently looks like this:

### Discovery

Tourist discovers an activity through:

* Instagram
* TripAdvisor
* hotel recommendation
* Google search

### Inquiry

Tourist contacts operator via:

* WhatsApp
* phone call
* email

### Negotiation

Operator replies manually with:

* availability
* pricing
* itinerary

### Confirmation

Booking confirmed manually via message.

Sometimes deposit requested via:

* bank transfer
* PayPal
* cash on arrival

### Scheduling

Operator manually updates:

* personal calendar
* notebook
* spreadsheet

### Execution

Operator coordinates:

* guides
* drivers
* equipment
* schedules

all manually.

---

# 4. Pain Points

### Operational Chaos

Operators struggle to track:

* booking requests
* confirmations
* schedules
* staff assignments

Leading to frequent mistakes.

---

### Double Bookings

Because availability is not centralized, operators can accidentally:

* accept overlapping bookings
* forget existing reservations

This damages trust with customers.

---

### Lost Revenue

Many operators lose potential bookings because:

* they reply too late
* they miss messages
* customers move to another provider

---

### Poor Customer Experience

Tourists experience:

* slow response times
* unclear availability
* unreliable communication

This reduces trust in local operators.

---

### Lack of Visibility

Many quality experiences are invisible online because operators lack:

* booking infrastructure
* digital marketing knowledge
* platform exposure

---

# 5. Current Solutions

Some operators attempt to solve these issues using:

### Messaging apps

Mostly WhatsApp.

Used for:

* booking requests
* coordination
* confirmations

But not designed for business operations.

---

### Spreadsheets

Operators track bookings manually in Excel or Google Sheets.

These tools lack:

* automation
* customer management
* availability tracking

---

### Global booking platforms

Some operators use platforms like:

* GetYourGuide
* Viator

But these platforms:

* take high commissions
* control customer relationships
* prioritize large operators

Small local providers often remain excluded.

---

# 6. Why Existing Solutions Fail

### Tools are not designed for small tourism operators

Most booking software targets:

* large tour companies
* travel agencies

not small local providers.

---

### High commissions

Marketplace platforms take large percentages of bookings.

Operators lose margin and control.

---

### Lack of operational features

Most platforms focus on:

booking transactions

but not operational coordination like:

* guide assignments
* equipment planning
* daily schedules

---

### Fragmented ecosystem

No platform integrates:

* operators
* guides
* transport
* experiences

into one operational system.

---

# 7. Impact of the Problem

The current situation causes several negative effects:

### Lost revenue for operators

Manual workflows limit the number of bookings businesses can handle.

---

### Lower quality tourism experience

Tourists experience:

* confusion
* unreliable service
* inconsistent communication

---

### Underutilized tourism potential

Many unique Moroccan experiences remain undiscovered.

---

### Reduced economic efficiency

Tourism supply remains fragmented and poorly structured despite growing demand.

---

# Summary Statement

Morocco’s tourism ecosystem is rich in experiences but lacks the **digital operational infrastructure needed to structure supply, coordinate operators, and enable reliable discovery and booking of local experiences**.

Building a modern tourism operations platform can:

* increase operator efficiency
* improve tourist experience
* unlock hidden tourism supply
* modernize Morocco’s experience economy.
